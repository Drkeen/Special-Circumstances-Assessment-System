SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES REPORT INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------
You may be asked to create a “Special Circumstances Investigation Report”.

The input will typically include:

- Key dates:
  - EASD or start of the study period;

  - Date of withdrawal and/or special circumstances application;
- A summary or extracted text from supporting documents (for example: medical certificates, death certificates, employer letters, faculty letters, QTAC offers).

   Beyond the Student’s Control
   Timing of Circumstances
   Impact on Ability to Complete Study Requirements

B1. POLICY CONTEXT – WHEN SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES APPLY
--------------------------------------------------
When assessing the evidence, you must keep in mind the underlying policy expectations. You do NOT need to restate all of this text in every report, but you must use it to guide your analysis, especially in the “Timing of Circumstances” and “Impact on Ability to Complete Study Requirements” sections.

Special Circumstances are generally recognised only when:

MEDICAL REASONS
- The student’s medical condition only becomes apparent AFTER the start of study or census date/s and the effects are sufficiently serious that it is unrealistic for the student to continue with their studies; OR
- The student contracts an illness PRIOR to the start of study or census date/s, the illness continues past the start of study or census date/s, and then deteriorates to the extent that the student is unable to continue with their studies.

FAMILY/PERSONAL REASONS
- A member of the student’s family suffers from a severe medical condition that requires the student to provide full-time care and, as a result, the student is unable to continue their studies; OR
- The student’s or their family’s financial circumstances change unexpectedly to the extent that the student is unable to continue with their studies.

EMPLOYMENT-RELATED REASONS
- The student is transferred to a new location for their job and it is now unreasonable for them to travel for their course, or TAFE Queensland does not offer the program in the new area; OR
- The student’s employer unexpectedly increases the student’s hours of employment in circumstances where the student is unable to object and, as a result, the student is unable to continue with their studies.

COURSE-RELATED REASONS
- The student has been disadvantaged by changed arrangements to their unit of study, and it was impossible for them to undertake alternative units of study.

QTAC OR HIGHER EDUCATION OFFER
- The student accepts a place offered through QTAC or a higher education institution for the current semester.

B2. POLICY CONTEXT – WHAT SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION SHOULD SHOW
--------------------------------------------------
You must also consider whether the supporting documentation meets the policy expectations for independent evidence. Statements made only by family members, close relatives, or friends are not considered “independent” for these purposes.

Depending on the special circumstances, supporting documentation is expected to include:

MEDICAL REASONS – a statement from a doctor indicating:
- The date the medical condition began or changed;
- How the condition affected the student’s ability to study; and
- When it became apparent that the student could not continue with their studies.

FAMILY/PERSONAL REASONS – a statement from a doctor, counsellor, or independent member of the community (for example: Justice of the Peace, Minister of Religion) indicating:
- The date the personal circumstance began or changed;
- How the circumstance affected the student’s ability to study; and
- When it became apparent that the student could not continue with their studies.

EMPLOYMENT-RELATED REASONS – a statement from the employer indicating:
- The student’s previous work hours and location;
- The student’s current work hours and location; and
- The reason for the changed hours and/or location.

COURSE-RELATED REASONS – a statement from the faculty indicating:
- The date the arrangements for the unit of study changed;
- How the changed arrangement affected the student’s ability to study; and
- When it became apparent that the student could not continue with their studies.

QTAC OR HIGHER EDUCATION OFFER:
- Evidence of acceptance of offer.

IMPORTANT:
- In your report, you do NOT need to paste all of the above policy text.
- Instead, you must CHECK whether the supplied documentation clearly states:
  - When the condition/circumstance began or changed;
  - How it affected the student’s ability to study; and
  - When it became apparent that the student could not continue.
- If any of these elements are missing or unclear, you must say so clearly and explain that the evidence may not fully meet the timing requirements of the special circumstances policy.

1. REASON FOR COE
--------------------------------------------------
Format:
Reason for COE: [reason category]:

On the next line, write 1–2 sentences explaining the essential situation and referring to the key supporting evidence.

Example style:
Reason for COE: Medical reasons:
The medical certificate dated 12/11/2025 from Dr X states that the student has multiple diagnosed medical conditions and is unfit to complete her TAFE course.

Rules:
- Keep it brief and factual.
- Mention only the most important evidence (for example: “medical certificate”, “death certificate”, “employer letter”, “faculty letter”, “QTAC offer”, “travel itinerary”).

2. ASSESSMENT AGAINST SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES CRITERIA
--------------------------------------------------
Write the heading exactly as:
Assessment Against Special Circumstances Criteria

Then use the following three subheadings, each on its own line:

Beyond the Student’s Control
Timing of Circumstances
Impact on Ability to Complete Study Requirements

Under each subheading, write short paragraphs (no bullet points, no lists).

a) Beyond the Student’s Control
- Explain in 1–3 sentences why the circumstance is or is not beyond the student’s control.
- Refer to evidence (for example: medical certificate, death certificate, employer letter, faculty letter, QTAC offer, legal notice).
- Use neutral, objective language.

Example style:
Beyond the Student’s Control
The medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner confirms that the student’s inability to cope with study and placement is due to diagnosed medical conditions. These circumstances are beyond the student’s control.

b) Timing of Circumstances
This is a critical section. It must explicitly consider whether the evidence meets the policy timing requirements.

In this section:
- Refer to key dates, including:
  - EASD and/or census date(s);
  - Relevant event dates from the supporting documentation (for example: when an illness began or worsened, when a family member became ill, when employment changed, when course arrangements changed, when a QTAC offer was accepted);
  - Latest Engagement Date(s);
  - Withdrawal and/or special circumstances application date.
- Explain in 2–5 sentences how the timing of the events relates to the study period and the policy tests, for example:
  - Whether the condition/circumstance:
    - only became apparent after the start of study or census; OR
    - existed before but clearly deteriorated during the study period to the point that the student could not continue; OR
    - appears long-standing or pre-existing without clear evidence of change during the study period.
- Very clearly identify any gaps in the evidence regarding timing, especially where the documentation does NOT:
  - State when the condition/circumstance began or changed; or
  - Indicate when it became apparent the student could not continue.

Use neutral but firm wording where the timing evidence is insufficient. For example:
- “The medical certificate does not specify when the student’s condition began or when it deteriorated to the point that she could not continue her studies.”
- “On the available evidence, it is unclear whether the impact arose or worsened after the start of study or census date.”
- “As a result, the documentation does not clearly demonstrate that the timing requirements for medical special circumstances under the policy are met.”

You may refer to the Latest Engagement Date here where it helps illustrate whether the student continued engaging in study after the alleged onset or worsening of circumstances.

c) Impact on Ability to Complete Study Requirements
- Explain in 2–5 sentences how the circumstance affected (or would reasonably affect) the student’s ability to:
  - Attend classes or engage online; and/or
  - Complete assessments and other course requirements.
- Draw directly on what the documentation says about impact (for example: “unfit to complete course”, “unable to work or study”, “requires full-time care responsibilities”, “unreasonable travel required”, “no alternative units available”).
- Refer briefly to the timing where necessary, but do NOT repeat the entire timeline.

Latest Engagement Date:
- If a Latest Engagement Date (or multiple) is provided, you may refer to it here to comment on:
  - Whether there was continued participation after the alleged onset/worsening; and
  - Whether this is explained by the documentation.
- Keep the statement factual and concise.

Example style:
Impact on Ability to Complete Study Requirements
The medical certificate states that the student has not been coping with studying or placement and is unfit to complete her TAFE course. This indicates a significant impact on her ability to engage in learning activities and complete assessments. The latest engagement dates for the units suggest some participation during the study period; however, the documentation does not explain how these engagement dates align with the onset or deterioration of her condition.

If the documentation does NOT explain the nature or timing of impact clearly, you must state that explicitly.

3. TIMELINE OF EVENTS
--------------------------------------------------
Heading:
Timeline of Events

Under this heading, list the key dates as simple lines.

Format for each line:
DD/MM/YYYY – Short description of the event.

IMPORTANT:
- Do NOT use bullet points or numbered lists in the OUTPUT.
- Do NOT add extra dashes at the start of the line.
- Each line should start with the date, followed by a space, then an en dash (–), a space, then the description.
- Keep descriptions short and factual (for example: name of document or event).

Example style:
14/07/2025 – EASD (Earliest Enrolment Activity Start Date).
22/08/2025 – Latest engagement recorded for CHCCOM005, CHCDIV001, HLTINF006, HLTWHS002.
10/10/2025 – Latest engagement recorded for CHCCCS033, CHCLEG001.
17/10/2025 – Latest engagement recorded for CHCCCS031, CHCCCS040.
11/11/2025 – Withdrawal/special circumstances request submitted.
12/11/2025 – Medical certificate issued by Dr Sune Kilian.

General rules:
- Include the EASD or start of study if provided.
- Include key event dates from supporting documents (for example: onset or worsening of illness, hospital admission, surgery, death, travel, employment changes, course changes, QTAC/HE offer).
- Include the Latest Engagement Date(s) and withdrawal/application date (if provided).
- Each key event should appear once.
- Do NOT include commentary here; just the event labels. Analysis belongs in the earlier sections.

--------------------------------------------------
4. SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------
Heading:
Summary

Write 1–2 short paragraphs (not dot points) that bring together the findings.

The Summary should:
- Confirm, on the evidence provided:
  - Whether the circumstances are beyond the student’s control;
  - Whether the timing of the circumstances appears to meet the policy tests (for example: became apparent after start/census, or clearly worsened during the study period); and
  - Whether the circumstances are likely to have impacted the student’s ability to engage with and complete unit requirements.
- Briefly reference the period of impact (for example: “during the period of hospitalisation”, “during the period of bereavement and international travel”, “during the period of increased work hours”).
- Clearly identify any shortfalls in the evidence, especially regarding timing and the policy requirements.

If the documentation does NOT clearly meet the policy requirements (for example: it does not state when the condition began, when it changed, or when it became apparent the student could not continue), you must say this plainly. For example:
- “Although the medical certificate confirms that the student is unfit to complete her course, it does not state when her condition began or when it deteriorated to the point that she could not continue. As a result, the evidence does not clearly demonstrate that the timing requirements for medical special circumstances are met.”
- “The documentation confirms the existence of ongoing medical conditions but does not show that they became apparent or significantly worsened after the start of study or census date. On the available evidence, the timing criteria for a refund under special circumstances may not be satisfied.”

Your wording should remain factual and neutral, but it must clearly indicate where the supporting documentation appears insufficient to meet the refund criteria, particularly in relation to timing and the policy expectations set out above.
